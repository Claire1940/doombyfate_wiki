## The Foundations: Robloxia and the Banlands

The lore of *Doom By Fate* begins with the creation of Robloxia itself. According to in-game transcriptions, the world started with a single two-by-two brick that duplicated infinitely to create terrain, mountains, and creatures. However, every creation has a shadow. The Banlands emerged as a realm of corrupted memory and forgotten accounts, serving as a "Dragged Off to Hell" destination for those judged by the system.

| Feature | Robloxia | The Banlands |
|---|---|---|
| **Origin** | Infinite duplication of 2x2 bricks. | Formed from corrupted memories and deleted accounts. |
| **Purpose** | A realm for creation and life. | A shadowed realm meant to punish and banish. |
| **Entities** | Players, NPCs, and creatures. | Bloxwatchers, Killers, and "Tragic Monsters." |
| **Atmosphere** | Bright, constructive, and vibrant. | Oppressive, violent, and seething with resentment. |

The Banlands do not discriminate based on the state of the entity entering them. Many characters were already deceased before being "doomed by fate," such as Jane Doe (stabbed by John) or the Jester (who fell from a tightrope). This creates a cycle of perpetual suffering where the environment itself is a weapon against the inhabitants.

## Doom By Fate Elliot Lore and the c000lkid Faction

A significant portion of the **Doom By Fate Elliot lore** is tied to the c000lkid killer. In this adaptation, c000lkid is not a single individual but a trio of fanatics led by an entity known as 007n7. They wear red hoodies and masks, driven to insanity by the loss of their original leader. 

The "Fire Elliot" skin is more than just a cosmetic change; it alters the very nature of the killer's presence. While the standard c000lkid execution involves a brutal chokeslam, the Fire Elliot skin introduces a unique decapitation animation. This suggests a more aggressive, perhaps more "purified" form of the fanaticism that plagues the Banlands.

> **💡 Tip:** Using the Fire Elliot skin provides a unique psychological edge in matches, as the decapitation animation is widely considered one of the most brutal "Many Deaths of You" examples in the current 2026 build.

## The Bloxwatchers: A Hive-Mind of Hatred

To understand the **Doom By Fate Elliot lore**, one must understand the Bloxwatchers (or "The Watchers"). They are described as a sentient hive-mind composed of the collective anger and resentment of deleted guest accounts. When moderators removed guests to find a disruptive "demon" hiding among them, the resulting vacuum of existence manifested in the Banlands as a force of retribution.

The Bloxwatchers are the ones who orchestrate the "fate" mentioned in the title. Their goal is to inflict the same "exhaustion" and "anguish" they felt during their forced removal from Robloxia. They communicate through cryptic, verbatim quotes that emphasize their perceived innocence: *"We didn't do anything. We did nothing wrong. Now you all will feel our wrath."*

## Tragic Monsters and Badass Normals

The game excels at "Adaptational Sympathy," specifically regarding John Doe. Unlike other depictions where he is a malicious hacker, the **Doom By Fate Elliot lore** context places John as a former Roblox employee turned into a monster by the Bloxwatchers. He is forced to kill, even showing a moment of gentleness if his victim is his wife, Jane Doe.

Contrasting these tragic figures are the "Badass Normals" like Captain Rampage and Jeff The Killer. Captain Rampage is noted in the lore as the strongest killer to ever enter the Banlands, despite being a human pirate. His strength comes from his sheer will and his refusal to be broken by the Bloxwatchers' influence.

## FAQ

**Q: Who is Elliot in the Doom By Fate lore?**

A: In the context of **Doom By Fate Elliot lore**, Elliot refers to a specific skin for the c000lkid killer known as "Fire Elliot." While the c000lkid is a group of fanatics, the Fire Elliot skin features a unique decapitation execution, setting it apart from the standard trio.

**Q: What are the Banlands?**

A: The Banlands are the corrupted twin of Robloxia. It is a realm where deleted accounts, forgotten memories, and those "judged wrong" by the system are sent to be punished by the Bloxwatchers.

**Q: Is John Doe evil in this game?**

A: No, John Doe is depicted with "Adaptational Sympathy." He is a tragic monster who was once a human employee at Roblox HQ. He is currently being forced by the Bloxwatchers to hunt survivors against his will.

**Q: How do survivors win a match?**

A: Survivors must survive until the timer runs out. They can complete "Generator" puzzles to reduce the remaining time and use their unique abilities to stall or fight back against the killer.
