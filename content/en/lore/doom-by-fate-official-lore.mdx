## The Killers: Masters of Brutality

The Killers in Doom by Fate are not just monsters; they are entities defined by their pasts and their specific methods of execution. Each Killer brings a unique mechanical "flavor" that reflects their narrative role.

### John Doe and the Corrupt Force
John Doe is perhaps the most iconic figure in the **doom by fate official lore**. Representing raw force and brutality, his lore centers around a "Corrupt Force" that allows him to plow through crowds like a battering ram. Unlike more subtle killers, John Doe’s presence is loud and physically dominating. His ability to throw survivors as projectiles suggests a history of immense, uncontrolled power that has been weaponized for the trial.

### The Old Man: The Master of the Pocket Dimension
The Old Man is arguably the most complex character in the game. His lore is heavily inspired by classic internet horror tropes, specifically focusing on a "Pocket Dimension."

## The Survivors: Resilience and Teamwork

The Survivors in Doom by Fate are not helpless victims. According to the **doom by fate official lore**, these individuals have adapted their skills to fight back against the encroaching darkness. Their abilities are survivalist incentives that encourage teamwork and tactical positioning.

### Jane Doe: The High-Tech Healer
Jane Doe’s lore is tied to her medkit laptop. Unlike traditional healers who might use magic or simple bandages, Jane Doe utilizes technology. Her healing rewards good positioning and timing, suggesting she is a strategist who thrives under pressure.

### Brawler and the Art of the Dodge
Brawler represents the physical resistance of the Survivor group. His "Pocket Sand" ability is more than just a stun; it is a defensive tool that allows him to dodge incoming swings entirely. This reflects a character who has spent a lifetime learning how to nullify damage through agility and grit.

### Soldier: The Protector
The Soldier’s lore is deeply rooted in the concept of "No Man Left Behind." He features a unique carry mechanic that allows him to physically save teammates. His "three blocks equals a punch" mechanic shows a disciplined approach to combat, turning defense into a viable offense.
