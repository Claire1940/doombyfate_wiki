## Map Overview: The Rampaged Ship

The Rampaged Ship is a massive, decimated pirate vessel that has been torn apart by an unknown force, though lore suggests Captain Rampage himself may be responsible. The map is defined by its four-deck structure, connected by various staircases that serve as both bottlenecks and escape routes. Most of the high-intensity gameplay occurs on the upper decks, where survivor spawns are most concentrated, but the lower decks offer valuable hiding spots for those looking to evade detection.

| Deck Level | Key Features | Strategic Importance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Upper Decks** | Main mast, open floor space, primary loops | High visibility; best area for long chases and looping. |
| **Middle Deck** | Tight corridors, generator spawns | Balanced area; good for losing the killer in turns. |
| **Lower Deck** | Dark corners, storage crates | Excellent for stealth; often overlooked by inexperienced killers. |

The vertical nature of the **doom by fate ship** means that sound cues are often misleading. A killer might sound like they are right behind you when they are actually on the deck directly above or below. Learning to distinguish these audio layers is a hallmark of an advanced player.

## Survivor Strategies for the Doom By Fate Ship

Playing as a survivor on the **doom by fate ship** requires a blend of aggression and caution. Because the map is relatively enclosed compared to open-forest maps, you must make every movement count. The stairs are your best friend and your worst enemy; while they allow for quick transitions between floors, they can also leave you cornered if you don't have a plan.

### Utilizing Environmental Hazards
The stairs on this map are particularly effective when combined with specific character abilities. For instance, if you are playing as Jester, placing bananas at the top of a staircase can send a pursuing killer tumbling all the way to the bottom. This tactic provides a significant window to recover stamina and break the line of sight.

### High-Power Looping Spots
The upper decks contain two of the most powerful looping areas in the game. These loops consist of obstacles that allow survivors to run in circles while maintaining a safe distance from the killer. However, these spots are not invincible.

> **Warning:** Be wary of Captain Rampage on the upper decks. His reload ability has a very short cooldown, allowing him to close gaps quickly and shut down loops that would normally work against other killers.

## Killer Tactics: Controlling the Vessel

For Killers, the **doom by fate ship** is a playground of opportunity, provided you can manage the map's multi-layered layout. The biggest challenge for killers is losing track of survivors who move between decks. If you find yourself losing a chase, it is often because the survivor has dropped down a level or used a staircase to reset their position.

### Tracking and Pressure
If you are playing a killer like John Doe, you must be diligent about checking the middle and bottom decks. Many survivors will attempt to hide in the lower hull once the generators on the upper decks are completed. 

*   **Patrol the Stairs:** Instead of following a survivor directly up the stairs, try to predict which deck they are heading to and cut them off by using an alternative route.
*   **Check the Corners:** The decimated parts of the ship provide many "blind spots" where survivors can crouch and wait for you to pass.
*   **Force the Drop:** Push survivors toward the edges of the upper decks. If they are forced to jump down to a lower level, they will often suffer a brief landing lag, giving you time to close the distance.

### Killer Spawn and Map Control
There are currently two known killer spawns on the Rampaged Ship. These spawns are strategically located to allow the killer to reach the upper decks quickly, where the majority of the initial survivor activity occurs. By establishing a presence early on the top deck, you can force survivors into the more dangerous, cramped lower sections of the ship.

## Advanced Gameplay: The Soldier Survivor

As shown in recent high-level matches, the Soldier class has become a formidable counter to killers on the **doom by fate ship**. The Soldier's ability to fight back changes the dynamic of the game from a pure chase to a tactical skirmish. 

1.  **Stun Management:** Use your bullets sparingly. A well-timed shot can stop a killer mid-lunge, especially in the narrow walkways of the middle deck.
2.  **Blocking:** Muscle memory is key. When a killer attempts a strike, blocking can prevent damage and create an opening for a counter-attack or an escape.
3.  **Team Coordination:** If multiple survivors are on the ship, the Soldier can act as a vanguard, drawing the killer's attention while others focus on generators.

> **💡 Tip:** When playing as the Soldier, try to engage the killer near the stairs. If you successfully stun them, the knockback can often send them down a level, giving your team massive amounts of breathing room.

## FAQ

**Q: How many generators spawn on the doom by fate ship?**

A: The number of generators can vary based on the game mode and player count, but they typically spawn across all four decks, with at least two usually appearing on the middle deck's cramped corridors.

**Q: What is the best way to escape a chase on the Rampaged Ship?**

A: The most effective way to escape is by using the stairs to change decks and then immediately hiding behind crates or in the shadows of the lower hull. Breaking line-of-sight is easier on this map than on flatter maps.

**Q: Is Captain Rampage stronger on this map?**

A: Yes, Captain Rampage has a distinct advantage on the **doom by fate ship** due to his reload mechanics and lore-based synergy with the environment. He can navigate the upper deck loops more effectively than most other killers.

**Q: Can the Soldier Survivor actually kill the killer on the ship?**

A: While the Soldier can hunt and stun killers, the goal is typically to incapacitate or drive them away rather than "kill" them in the traditional sense, allowing the team to complete objectives safely.
