## Artful: The Master of Illusions

Artful is a French magician whose career ended in a tragic public humiliation. Driven by rage, he now uses his magical prowess to "repurpose" his audience. He is a technical killer who excels at capturing survivors and using them against their own teammates.

### Artful's Ability Kit
*   **Swing:** A standard melee attack with his wand.
*   **Implement:** Utilizes magical tools to hinder survivor movement.
*   **Copyright:** A trap-based ability that punishes survivors for interacting with specific objects.
*   **Repurpose:** Artful reaches forward with his wand; if he connects, he "pockets" the survivor, eventually turning them into a mindless puppet.

## Pursuer: The Apex Predator

The Pursuer is arguably the most terrifying Slasher for survivors due to its ability to vanish from sight. Lore suggests it was a government-released experiment that now hunts for "fresh meat." It relies on a "Stalk" and "Howl" loop to locate and eliminate targets before they realize the threat is near.

| Ability | Effect | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cleave** | Heavy sword strike | Use after a successful Stalk for maximum impact. |
| **Stalk** | Invisibility | Activate this to approach generators without being seen. |
| **Howl** | Map-wide reveal | Use this when you lose track of survivors in high-grass areas. |

## Badware and Kill Droid: The Tech Terror

These two killers represent the mechanical horrors of Doom by Fate. While Badware is a digital virus inhabiting a prototype shell, Kill Droid is a repurposed security bot with a corrupted directive to "eliminate all criminals"—which, in its logic, means everyone.

*   **Badware Strategy:** You must place computers strategically around the map. By infecting these terminals, you can trigger explosions when survivors attempt to repair them or pass nearby. It is considered one of the hardest killers to play due to the prototype body's lower base movement speed.
*   **Kill Droid Strategy:** This Slasher is perfect for players who enjoy "bullet hell" mechanics. With rockets, jetpacks, and mines, you can pressure survivors from a distance, making it nearly impossible for them to find a safe spot to heal.

## Harkin: The Construct of Silence

Harkin is a being from another dimension made of sand and packed dust. Unlike the more "evil" killers, Harkin kills out of a desperate need for silence, fearing that loud noises will attract even larger, deadlier constructs from her home realm.

| Mechanic | How it Works |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Noise Meter** | Fills up as survivors run, scream, or use items nearby. |
| **Agitation** | Once the meter is full, Harkin enters an enraged state with increased speed. |
| **Tangle** | Roots survivors in place using earthen elements. |
| **Emulate** | A powerful area-of-effect attack used to clear out groups. |

> **Warning:** Survivors like the "Soldier" can block your attacks. If you see a Soldier raising their arms, delay your M1 attack to avoid being staggered and losing your momentum.

## FAQ

**Q: Who is the best killer for beginners in Doom by Fate?**

A: Kill Droid is generally recommended for new players. Its infinite ammo and area-of-effect rockets allow you to contribute to the match even if your melee tracking isn't perfect yet.

**Q: How do I counter the Soldier's block ability?**

A: The Soldier's block only lasts 2-3 seconds. If you telegraph your attack and they block, you will be staggered. The best strategy is to walk up to them, wait for the animation to end, and then strike while their ability is on its 7-second cooldown.

**Q: Can all killers go invisible like the Pursuer?**

A: No, invisibility is a unique trait of the Pursuer. Other killers must rely on line-of-sight breaking and map knowledge to surprise survivors.

**Q: Where can I find the full doom by fate wiki killers list?**
