## The Medical Reality of Seizures

To understand why the story fell apart, it is important to look at the medical facts surrounding epilepsy. Seizures triggered by visual stimuli, known as photosensitive epilepsy, are serious but rarely result in immediate death during a single episode unless accompanied by secondary trauma (like a fall) or prolonged status epilepticus.

1.  **First Aid:** You should never put anything in a person's mouth or perform CPR during a seizure. Instead, you should cushion their head and turn them on their side.
2.  **Hospital Access:** Modern hospitals use strict key-card access for surgical wards. A 13-year-old sneaking into an active operating room unnoticed is virtually impossible.
3.  **Post-Mortem Procedures:** When a patient passes away in surgery, there are extensive legal and medical protocols, including time-of-death declarations and immediate paperwork, not just a sudden cessation of movement by the staff.
