## The "Carry" Gimmick: Advanced Support Perks

Perhaps the most unique aspect of the Soldier’s kit is the ability to physically carry teammates. This isn't just a metaphorical carry; the Soldier literally picks up a wounded ally and flings them over his shoulder. This maneuver is a literal lifesaver when healers like Jane are out of resources or when a teammate is trapped in a corner.

When you are carrying a teammate, you essentially act as a tank. You take the brunt of the attention while moving the vulnerable player to a safer location, such as a second-story ledge or a healing station. However, this perk requires consent; the teammate must accept the carry, which can be difficult in fast-paced solo-queue matches.

## Combat Strategy and Hitbox Awareness

One of the most frequent complaints among the community involves the killer's hitbox. Many killers possess hitboxes that extend several feet beyond their character model. To counter this, you must use your **doom by fate perks** preemptively rather than reactively.

1.  **Anticipate the Swing:** If a killer is within two player-lengths of you, they are likely already in range to land a hit. Activate your block early.
2.  **Verticality is Your Friend:** Use stairs and ledges. The Soldier can shoot killers off ladders or stairs, causing them to fall and resetting their chase momentum.
3.  **Malice Management:** Keep an eye on your Malice level. High Malice increases the likelihood of the killer targeting you specifically. Use your stuns to "drop" aggro onto other players if you are near death.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Projectiles often cannot be blocked. If you are facing a killer with ranged darts or magical attacks, your block charges will be wasted. Focus on line-of-sight (LoS) maneuvers instead.

## FAQ

**Q: What are the best doom by fate perks for a solo player?**

A: If you are playing solo, the Soldier's "Block" and "Shotgun" perks are the most reliable. They allow you to protect yourself without needing to rely on teammates for protection. However, the "Carry" mechanic is less effective in solo play as it requires coordination that random teammates might not provide.

**Q: Can the Soldier block all types of killer attacks?**

A: No. While the Soldier can block most melee strikes and physical lunges, he generally cannot block projectiles like darts or area-of-effect (AoE) magical spells. It is best to use environmental cover for these types of attacks.

**Q: How do I trigger the Soldier's counterattack?**

A: You must successfully block three attacks in a row without the block cooldown triggering. Once the third block is successful, your character will automatically (or via a specific follow-up input) perform a counterattack that stuns or heavily slows the killer.

**Q: Why won't my teammates let me carry them?**

A: The carry mechanic requires the other player to "accept" the interaction. Many players are either unaware of the mechanic or prefer to maintain control over their own movement. It is often helpful to use the in-game communication system to signal your intent before attempting a carry.
