## The Complete Killer Roster (2026)

The current roster offers a mix of classic internet creepypasta icons and original characters. Each requires a different amount of "Tix" (the in-game currency) to unlock, with the exception of the default starter.

### C00LKID (The Default)
C00LKID is the entry-point for most players. Despite being free, he remains a highly viable threat due to his ability to ignore physical obstacles with his projectiles.

*   **M1 (Forward Slash):** A standard melee attack for close-range pressure.
*   **Q (Dual Blade):** Throws two blades that travel through walls. This is your primary tool for flushing out survivors hiding in rooms or behind cover.
*   **E (Lasciarte):** A dash-and-slice move that inflicts a 2-second stun. Perfect for stopping a survivor mid-loop.
*   **R (Velocity Strike):** A high-damage punch with massive knockback. Use this to clear survivors away from objectives or into hazards.
*   **T (Speed Hack):** Grants a temporary movement speed boost and ESP (Extra Sensory Perception), allowing you to see survivor outlines through walls.

### Jeff The Killer (Cost: 1,000 Tix)
Jeff is the quintessential stealth killer. He relies on positioning and the element of surprise rather than raw tankiness.

*   **M1 (Slash/Stab):** A two-hit combo. Notably, this deals significantly higher damage if you manage to hit a survivor from behind (Backstab).
*   **Q (Murderous Intent):** Jeff becomes invisible and silent. While your movement speed is slightly reduced, you can navigate the map undetected. Be careful: an "eye" icon appears for survivors when you are very close, revealing your presence.
*   **E (Sweet Dreams):** A versatile grab. From the front, Jeff stabs the survivor repeatedly before ragdolling them. From the back, he delivers a single powerful stab followed by a kick.
*   **R (Bloodthirst):** Grants ESP on a specific survivor and enters a "rush" state where Jeff can slash through multiple targets with increased aggression.

### Captain Rampage (Cost: 2,250 Tix)
Captain Rampage introduces a unique resource management mechanic called **Shark Prey Rounds**. He is one of the few killers who cannot ragdoll survivors, but he makes up for it with sheer offensive pressure.

*   **Shark Prey Rounds:** You have a magazine of 6 bullets. Your M1 attacks consume these rounds to deal empowered damage.
*   **R (Tiger Slash):** This move reloads your Shark Prey Rounds. It also provides iFrames (invincibility frames) and temporary ESP, making it a great defensive tool.
*   **E (Tidal Cleaver):** A charged-up slash that hits every survivor in a wide path.
*   **Q (Shatter Slash):** A quick offensive strike used to maintain pressure between reloads.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Managing your Shark Prey Rounds is vital. Never engage a group of survivors with an empty magazine, as your damage output drops significantly without empowered M1s.

### John Doe (Cost: 2,750 Tix)
John Doe is the most expensive standard killer and functions as an unstoppable juggernaut. While he has a lower base movement speed, he possesses a passive immunity to staggering, meaning survivors cannot easily interrupt his momentum.

*   **Q (Corroded Wave):** John Doe creates a trail of black sludge on the floor. Survivors who touch it are slowed and damaged, while John Doe gains a speed boost while standing on it.
*   **E (Command Grab):** A standard grab that inflicts a temporary ragdoll, allowing you to set up for heavier follow-up attacks.
*   **R (Corrupt Force):** A charging move where John Doe slams into survivors. If you slam a survivor into a wall, the damage is greatly increased.

## Best Practices for Killers
To succeed in high-level play, you must look beyond individual abilities and focus on map pressure.

1.  **Listen for Audio Cues:** Survivors often make distinct sounds when interacting with objects or attempting to stun you. Use these cues to dodge incoming CC (Crowd Control).
2.  **Target Priority:** Identify the "loopers" on the survivor team. If one survivor is particularly good at wasting your time, redirect your focus to those doing generators or objectives to force the loopers into the open.
3.  **Use Terrain:** Many killers, like John Doe and C00LKID, have abilities that interact with the environment. Always try to position survivors between yourself and a wall to maximize knockback damage or "Corrupt Force" slams.
4.  **Monitor Cooldowns:** Don't waste your high-impact moves (like Velocity Strike or Bloodthirst) on a single survivor if a group is nearby. Save your big hits for when they can provide the most value.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid chasing a single survivor for too long. In Doom By Fate, time is the survivors' greatest ally. If you can't land a hit within 30 seconds, rotate to a different area of the map.

## FAQ

**Q: Who is the best character in the Doom By Fate all killers guide?**

A: There is no single "best" killer, as it depends on your playstyle. John Doe is excellent for players who want to ignore stuns, while Jeff the Killer is superior for those who enjoy stealth. C00LKID is widely considered the most balanced for all situations.

**Q: How do I earn Tix quickly to unlock new killers?**

A: The most reliable way to earn Tix is by winning rounds as either a survivor or a killer. Additionally, keep an eye out for promotional codes released by the developers, which often provide a significant Tix boost.

**Q: Can survivors stun every killer?**

A: Most killers can be stunned or staggered by survivor items and environmental hazards. However, John Doe has a passive ability that prevents him from being staggered, making him uniquely resistant to survivor counter-play.

**Q: Are there any upcoming killers being added soon?**

A: The roster is updated periodically. While 1x1x1x1 is a frequently discussed entity in the community, players should check the official game wiki and social media channels for the latest confirmed additions to the roster.
