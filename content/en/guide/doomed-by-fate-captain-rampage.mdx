Before diving into the complex mechanics of his blade work, it is important to understand the baseline capabilities of this killer. Captain Rampage is not a "tank" or a "trapper"; he is a pure aggressor. His stats reflect a character designed to stay in the chase until the job is done.

## Mastering the Doomed by Fate Captain Rampage Kit

The core of the Captain's power lies in his unique resource management system: **Sharks Prey Rounds**. You begin every match with six rounds, which are displayed on the right side of your interface. These rounds are not for a gun, but rather to empower your melee strikes and special abilities.

### Passive: Sharks Prey Rounds
Your basic M1 attack changes significantly depending on your ammo count.
*   **Without Rounds:** You deal 25 damage to a single target.
*   **With Rounds:** You perform a lunging swing that deals 30 damage. Crucially, this empowered version can hit multiple survivors at once, making it devastating in grouped encounters. Each successful or missed swing in this state consumes one round.

### The Art of the Reload (R)
Reloading is not just a downtime mechanic; it is a tactical tool. When you press 'R', you refill your rounds, but you also gain:
1.  **Speed Boost:** A 1-second burst of movement speed.
2.  **Invincibility Frames (i-frames):** During the reload animation, you are immune to damage and stuns.
3.  **Aura Reading:** Reloading allows you to see survivor locations nearby, making it an excellent tool for "aura farming" and tracking.

> **💡 Tip:** Use the reload animation to parry incoming stuns from survivors. The i-frames end exactly when the animation does, so timing is critical.

## Ability Breakdown: Shatter Slash & Tidal Cleaver

To excel as a **doomed by fate captain rampage** main, you must know exactly how much ammo to spend on your abilities. Mismanaging your rounds will leave you with significantly lower damage output.

### Shatter Slash (Q)
This is your primary gap-closer and crowd-control tool.
*   **Single Press:** Lunges forward, dealing 15 damage (with ammo) or 10 damage (without). It applies a 2-second slowness effect.
*   **Double Tap:** If you land the first hit and immediately press Q again, the Captain lunges forward and pushes the survivor away. This deals 20 damage and costs two rounds total. This is best used to pin survivors against environmental obstacles.

### Tidal Cleaver (E)
This ability is holdable and changes its properties based on the charge duration. It is your most versatile tool for both damage and utility.

| Version | Charge Requirement | Damage (With Ammo) | Effects |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Quick Tap** | Instant | 15 Damage | Short range slash; 1s slow effect. |
| **Medium Hold** | White Highlight | 15 Damage | Dashing forward slash; 1s slow effect. |
| **Full Charge** | Max Highlight | 25 Damage | Leap into air and slam; applies **Burn** (1 dmg/3s). |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Using Tidal Cleaver without ammo drops the damage significantly (e.g., the slam drops from 25 to 15). Always check your round counter before committing to a full charge.

## Advanced Combos and Tactical Play

Mastering the **doomed by fate captain rampage** requires more than just spamming buttons. You need to chain your abilities to ensure survivors cannot recover.

### The "Wall-Pin" Execution
This is the most reliable combo for securing a kill:
1.  **Shatter Slash (Q) x2:** Use the double-tap version to launch a survivor into a wall.
2.  **Tidal Cleaver (E):** Time this right as they are recovering from the ragdoll state. Do not hit them while they are fully ragdolled, as the 'E' ability may not register.
3.  **Basic Attack (M1):** Follow up with an empowered swing for 30 damage.
4.  **Reload (R):** Immediately reload to gain the speed boost and stay on top of them if they try to flee.

### Mobility and Map Control
If you find yourself out of stamina, don't stop the chase. Use **Shatter Slash** to lunge forward; it covers significant ground and stalls the survivor's stamina regeneration, making them easier to catch. Additionally, the full-charge **Tidal Cleaver** allows you to leap over common looping spots, cutting off escape routes that would normally frustrate other killers.

## Pro Tips for Captain Rampage Players

*   **Generator Pressure:** Use a full-charge Tidal Cleaver on survivors working on generators. The sudden leap and high burst damage (plus burn) can delete a massive chunk of their HP before they can react.
*   **Pre-Chase Prep:** If you aren't currently in a chase, always hit your reload. You never want to start an encounter with only one or two rounds.
*   **Counter-Stunning:** Survivors will often try to time stuns when you get close. Since Tidal Cleaver has built-in i-frames, you can use it right before a stun lands to completely ignore the effect and land a hit simultaneously.
*   **Aura Tracking:** If you lose track of a survivor in high-grass or complex buildings, the reload animation's aura-reading capability is your best friend. It essentially acts as a localized radar.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get more rounds for the doomed by fate captain rampage?**

A: You must use the Reload (R) ability. Unlike some characters who replenish ammo over time, Captain Rampage requires a manual reload which grants a speed boost and invincibility frames during the animation.

**Q: Can Captain Rampage hit multiple survivors at once?**

A: Yes, but only if you have at least one Sharks Prey round available. His empowered M1 lunge has a wider hit area that can damage multiple targets, whereas his empty-clip M1 is strictly single-target.

**Q: What is the best way to use Tidal Cleaver?**

A: The best way is to use the full-charge version for the burn effect and high damage. However, the quick tap is excellent for "locking down" a survivor who is right on top of you, ensuring they cannot dodge the subsequent M1.

**Q: Is Captain Rampage good for beginners?**

A: He has a moderate learning curve. While his high HP makes him forgiving, successfully managing his ammo and timing the i-frames on his reload requires practice. He is an excellent choice for players who prefer aggressive, fast-paced gameplay.
