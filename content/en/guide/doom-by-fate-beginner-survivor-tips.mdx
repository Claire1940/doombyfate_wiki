## Understanding Movement and Stealth

In Doom By Fate, your primary defense is your ability to remain unseen. While it might be tempting to sprint everywhere to complete objectives faster, noise is your greatest enemy. Every action you take—from running to vaulting windows—creates sound cues that killers can use to track your location.

| Movement Type | Noise Level | Speed | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Crouching** | Near Silent | Very Slow | Hiding behind low cover or avoiding detection perks. |
| **Walking** | Low | Moderate | Standard movement when the killer's location is unknown. |
| **Sprinting** | High | Fast | Escaping a chase or reaching a teammate in critical need. |
| **Vaulting** | High | Instant | Breaking line of sight during an active pursuit. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always look behind you while running. Mastering the "back-look" camera allows you to see how close the killer is and which direction they are swinging, giving you a better chance to dodge.

## Objective Management: The Path to Escape

The core objective for survivors is to complete environmental tasks—often referred to as "generators" or "seals"—to power the exit gates. Beginners often make the mistake of grouping up on a single objective. While this speeds up that specific task, it makes the team vulnerable to area-of-effect attacks and allows the killer to pressure multiple people at once.

1.  **Spread Out:** At the start of the match, players should identify different objectives to work on. This forces the killer to traverse the entire map, wasting their time.
2.  **Check for "Heat":** Some killers have abilities that increase the "Heat" or "Tension" in an area. If you notice environmental changes like flickering lights or rising embers, the killer is likely nearby.
3.  **Prioritize Center Objectives:** Objectives in the middle of the map are the most dangerous because they are easy for the killer to patrol. Completing these early makes the late-game much easier for survivors.

| Objective Stage | Priority | Risk Level | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Early Game** | High | Medium | Focus on the "Center" objectives to break the killer's patrol path. |
| **Mid Game** | Medium | High | Heal injured teammates and keep at least two objectives progressing. |
| **Late Game** | Critical | Very High | Stealth is mandatory. Do not reveal your location until the exit is powered. |

### Dealing with the Sleep Mechanic
Some killers can force survivors into a "Dream State." In this state, the environment becomes distorted, and the killer may become invisible or gain a teleportation ability. To counter this, look for "Wake Up" stations or have a teammate perform a "Snap Out of It" action.

### Managing Heat and Rampage
Killers who utilize "Heat" often leave trails or burn objectives. If you stay in a heated zone for too long, your character may scream, revealing your location. When a killer enters a "Rampage" mode, they gain increased movement speed and breaking power. During a Rampage, your only goal should be to hide or use high-walled loops to waste their power duration.

> **Warning:** Never attempt to "bully" a killer during their Rampage phase. Their hit recovery is significantly reduced, making traditional stuns less effective.

## Advanced Evasion: The Art of the Loop

"Looping" is the process of leading the killer around a structure (like a pallet or a window) to buy time for your team. This is the most difficult skill to master but the most rewarding.

*   **Hug the Walls:** When running around an object, stay as close to the collision box as possible. This minimizes the distance you travel compared to the killer.
*   **Don't Drop Pallets Early:** A pallet is a finite resource. If you "pre-drop" every pallet, the map will become a dead zone by the end of the match. Try to take at least two laps around a loop before dropping the pallet.
*   **The "360" Spin:** If the killer is right behind you and about to swing, try spinning your character in a tight circle while moving toward the killer. This can occasionally cause the killer's auto-aim to miss.

## Team Coordination and Altruism

Doom By Fate is a team game. If you play selfishly, the killer will pick you off one by one. Altruism—the act of helping others—is actually a survival strategy. If a teammate is on a hook or captured, the killer's attention is divided. This is your window to either rescue them or finish the final objectives.

**The "One-For-One" Rule:**
If the killer is "camping" a hooked survivor, do not rush in blindly. Wait for a second teammate to arrive. One person can distract the killer while the other performs the rescue. If you try to do it alone, you will likely end up on the hook yourself, resulting in a "one-for-one" trade that benefits the killer.

## FAQ

**Q: What are the most important doom by fate beginner survivor tips for someone who keeps dying first?**

A: The most important tip is to focus on stealth and line-of-sight. If you hear the heartbeat or terror radius, stop what you are doing and find a hiding spot immediately. Do not wait until you see the killer to start moving. Additionally, avoid running in open fields where you are an easy target for projectiles.

**Q: How do I know which objectives to prioritize?**

A: Always prioritize the "three-gen" situation. This happens when the last three objectives are very close to each other, making it easy for the killer to defend them. Try to complete one of those three objectives early in the match to prevent the killer from locking down the final stage of the game.

**Q: Can I fight back against the killer?**

A: Most survivors cannot "kill" the killer. Your "fighting" consists of using environmental hazards like pallets, flashlights, or specific character abilities to stun or blind them. These are temporary measures designed to help you escape a chase, not to eliminate the threat entirely.

**Q: What should I do if I am the last survivor left?**

A: When you are the last one remaining, the "Trapdoor" or "Emergency Hatch" will open somewhere on the map. Listen for a distinct humming or wind-like sound. This is your only way out if the exit gates haven't been powered yet. Avoid running, as the killer will be patrolling the hatch and the gates aggressively.
