## Overview of The Old Man (SCP-106)

SCP-106, often referred to as The Old Man, is a terrifying humanoid entity characterized by advanced decomposition and a signature, unsettling smile. In the world of *Doom By Fate*, he stands out not only for his grotesque appearance but also for being the first character to feature fully articulated fingers, adding a layer of uncanny realism to his model. 

His playstyle revolves around "The Long Game." While other killers might seek immediate eliminations, SCP-106 thrives on repeated captures. By dragging survivors into his Pocket Dimension, he applies a stacking debuff that makes escape increasingly difficult. This makes him a nightmare for survivors who favor long-term objective play, such as repairing generators.

## Deep Dive: The Pocket Dimension Stacking Mechanic

The most unique aspect of the **doom by fate scp 106** gameplay is the stacking damage within his domain. Every time a specific survivor is pulled into the Pocket Dimension, the difficulty of their escape increases, and the damage they take scales upward.

| Capture Count | Damage Multiplier | Escape Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1st Capture** | Base Damage | Standard |
| **2nd Capture** | +1 Damage per tick | Increased |
| **3rd Capture** | +2 Damage per tick | High |
| **4th+ Capture** | +3 Damage per tick | Near Impossible |

This mechanic encourages "targeting" or "tunneling" a specific high-value survivor. If you can successfully capture the same player three times, they are unlikely to survive the fourth encounter, regardless of their current health. This makes SCP-106 one of the best killers for removing the most skilled survivor from the opposing team early in the match.

## Pro Strategies for Playing SCP-106

To maximize your efficiency with the **doom by fate scp 106** kit, you must move away from the "brute force" approach used by characters like the Brawler. Instead, adopt a predator's mindset.

### 1. Prioritize Objective Campers
Survivors working on generators are your primary targets. Because they are stationary and often focused on skill checks, they are highly susceptible to being pulled into the Pocket Dimension. Use **Portal Latch** to appear behind a generator and snatch a survivor before they can react.

### 2. The "Hit and Fade" Tactic
You do not always need to commit to a full chase. Applying the corrosion effect with a **Claw** strike and then disappearing into a portal forces survivors to spend time healing. This slows down the game's pace, giving you more opportunities to set up captures.

### 3. Managing the Exit Gates
When survivors are trapped in the Pocket Dimension, they will eventually emerge at a predictable location. Smart survivors will wait for you to exit so they can stun you. To counter this, use **Sinister Grasp** immediately upon exiting to catch them off guard before they can activate their abilities.

## Survivor Counters: How to Beat the Old Man

## FAQ

**Q: How do I escape the Pocket Dimension when playing against SCP-106?**

A: To escape, you must navigate through the dark hallways and find a functional door. Some doors lead deeper into the dimension, while others return you to the map. If you have a stun ability, use it the moment you see The Old Man emerge to guarantee a safe path to the exit.

**Q: Does the corrosion damage from doom by fate scp 106 stack with other effects?**

A: Yes, the corrosion damage is unique and can stack with other environmental hazards or damage-over-time effects from other killers in team-based modes. However, its primary power comes from the +1 damage increase applied each time you are recaptured.

**Q: Is SCP-106 considered a "Stealth" or "Power" killer?**

A: He is a hybrid. While his abilities allow for high damage output, his most effective playstyle is stealth-based. Catching survivors unaware is the only way to reliably trigger the Pocket Dimension capture.

**Q: Can the Soldier's shield block the Pocket Dimension pull?**

A: No, the Pocket Dimension pull is a grab mechanic rather than a projectile. While a shield might mitigate the initial **Claw** damage, it will not prevent you from being dragged into the domain if you are caught unaware.
