## The Corrupted Cleric: Lore and Appearance

Before his fall, The Priest served as a beacon of calm and comfort in the church of Redcliff. His transformation began innocently enough, lured by the forbidden knowledge contained within "The Necrobloxicon." Obsessed, he delved deeper into its hellish verses, leading to a grotesque metamorphosis. A bright halo, a symbol of his corruption rather than divine blessing, appeared above his head, and four giant, fleshy red arms emerged from his back, moving with unsettling squelches. He now wears a dark robe and a top hat adorned with glow sticks, his eyes obscured by its brim, a sinister smile perpetually etched on his face. His glowstick necklace replaces any traditional holy symbol, marking his complete deviation from his former self.

Driven by the Necrobloxicon's influence, his mind twisted, convincing him that all living creatures are "dirty sinners" deserving punishment. The Bloxwatcher, an entity he mistook for God, offered him the divine purpose of purging the realm. Without hesitation, he embraced his new role, uttering "Purgamini. Nolite resistere." – Cleanse them. Do not resist.

## The Priest's Unique Playstyle: Target and Combo

The Priest is fundamentally a target and combo killer. His gameplay revolves around identifying a single survivor, isolating them, and relentlessly pursuing them with a series of chained abilities. While his individual attacks may have a smaller damage output compared to other killers, his strength lies in his ability to repeatedly hit targets and control engagements through his AOE capabilities. New players often struggle with The Priest due to the precision required to land his moves and the need for advanced baiting skills. Success with The Priest often means focusing down one survivor at a time, effectively preventing them from participating in the game.

His average speed is compensated by his ability to catch survivors off guard with his combos. He also possesses a unique passive trait: he can ignore banana peels, rendering him immune to slipping. This allows for more aggressive pathing and pursuit without worrying about environmental hazards.

### Effective Combo Routes

The synergy between The Priest's abilities is where his true power lies. The goal is to keep survivors locked down and taking damage.

| Combo Sequence                                         | Effect & Purpose                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| :----------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Snap > M1 > Crucifixion > M1 > Pray (on top) > M1** | A foundational combo for single-target elimination. Snap initiates and slows, Crucifixion deals burst damage and can reposition, Pray follows up with AOE, and M1s fill the gaps.                                             |
| **Crucifixion > M1 > Pray > M1 > Snap > M1**           | An alternative opener if Crucifixion lands first. This can be effective for surprising a survivor or punishing a Brawler attempting to intervene.                                                                          |
| **Shriek (or Pray) to deny stun > Crucifixion**        | If a Brawler attempts to stun you, immediately use Shriek or Pray to deal damage and potentially interrupt their attempt, then follow up with Crucifixion to toss them away and resume chasing your primary target.           |
| **Snap > Shriek (or Pray)**                            | A quick burst combo for dealing damage and maintaining pressure, especially useful when a survivor is slowed by Snap or caught in a tight space.                                                                          |

> **💡 Tip:** Always aim to integrate your basic M1 attacks between abilities. This maximizes your damage per second and ensures you're constantly applying pressure. The Priest's combos are fluid, and successful players adapt their sequence based on the survivor's movement and positioning.

## Strategic Gameplay: How to Play The Priest

Playing **doom by fate the priest** effectively requires a clear strategy and an understanding of his strengths and weaknesses.

### Target Prioritization

The Priest excels at eliminating specific survivor types due to his large hitbox and combo potential.

| Survivor Type | Priority | Reason                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| :------------ | :------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Jesters**     | **High**   | Your large hitbox makes it easy for them to land slowing abilities like Pie. Eliminating them early removes a significant threat to your chase potential.                                                                    |
| **Jane Does**   | **High**   | Similar to Jesters, Jane Doe's syringe can be easily landed on your larger frame, slowing you down. Focus them to maintain your mobility advantage.                                                                         |
| **Weaker/Isolated Survivors** | **High**   | The Priest's core playstyle is to prevent one survivor from playing. Target those who are alone, have low health, or are less skilled at evading to secure early eliminations and snowball your advantage. |
| **Soldiers/Brawlers** | **Medium** | While they can protect survivors, your AOE attacks (Shriek, Pray) and ability to toss with Crucifixion can deter them. Punish them if they try to interfere, but don't get distracted from your primary target.     |

### Maximizing Your Advantages

*   **Relentless Pursuit**: Once you've chosen a target, commit. Use Snap to locate and slow, then chain into Crucifixion and Pray to keep them locked down. The goal is to "prevent them from playing the game" until they are eliminated.
*   **Exploit Your Hitbox**: While sometimes a hindrance, your larger hitbox means your AOE abilities (Shriek, Pray) have a wider effective range. Use this to catch multiple survivors or pressure those trying to assist your target.
*   **Stamina Advantage**: The Priest has a smaller "In Chase" range. If you are stunned by a Brawler and they manage to create enough distance, you might exit the chase range before losing significant stamina. This can give you a massive stamina advantage for resuming the pursuit.
*   **Wall Combos**: The Priest "can (and will) put you in the wall." Use Crucifixion to pin survivors against environmental obstacles, making it harder for them to escape and easier for you to land subsequent hits.
