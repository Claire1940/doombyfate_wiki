## Survivor Classes and Playstyles

In *Doom By Fate*, survivors are categorized into specific roles that define their utility during a match. While every player needs to contribute to generator repairs, your chosen class determines how you interact with the killer and your teammates. Understanding these archetypes is essential for building a balanced four-person squad.

### The Survivalist Role
Survivalists are the backbone of the team’s progression. These characters are designed to focus on the primary objective: repairing generators (often referred to as "gens"). Their kits usually include tools for self-preservation and information gathering, allowing them to avoid the killer while keeping the escape clock ticking.

### The Sentinel Role
Sentinels are the frontline defenders. If a Survivalist is the "brain" of the team, the Sentinel is the "shield." Their primary goal is to interrupt the killer's momentum through stuns, displacement, and peel mechanics. By drawing the killer's attention and extending chases, Sentinels buy precious time for their teammates to finish objectives.

## Essential Survival Strategies

Winning a match as a survivor isn't just about running away; it's about efficient resource management and spatial awareness. The most successful **doom by fate survivors** utilize the terrain and audio cues to negate the killer's advantages.

### Generator Management and Grouping
While it is tempting to stick together for safety, "gen rushing" is often more effective when players split up strategically. If the killer is chasing one player, the other three should be on separate generators to maximize pressure. However, if you are facing a "Stealth" type killer, sticking in pairs is safer to ensure someone is available for a quick stun or rescue.

### Utilizing Terrain and Loops
The maps in *Doom By Fate* are filled with "loop spots"—areas where high vaults or tight corners allow survivors to create distance. 
1.  **Identify High-Wall Loops:** These block the killer's line of sight, making it easier to perform a "mind game" and change direction.
2.  **Save Pallets:** Do not drop pallets immediately. Try to loop the area at least twice before using the stun to maximize the killer's wasted time.
3.  **Listen for Audio Cues:** Killers often have distinct sounds before using an ability. Learning these cues allows you to dodge stuns or projectiles effectively.

**Q: What should I do if the killer is camping a generator?**

A: If a killer is "proxy camping" a near-complete generator, have one Sentinel draw their attention away while the remaining **doom by fate survivors** focus on completing other objectives elsewhere on the map. Forcing the killer to move is better than feeding them kills at a single point.
